## Abstract

N-BEATS, Oreshkin et al. (2019), broke new ground in univariate time series forecasting by being one of the first pure deep learning algorithms that was competitive with traditional statistical and hybrid statistical/deep learning techniques. Their contribution was signifigant because data could be fed into their models unmodified -- without scaling, normalization, or hand-crafting features. Additionally, they pionered the use of neural basis expansion in which the network learned coefficients of a frozen basis. However, their solution came at an extreme cost, a single N-BEATS model could often reach 25M-45M parameters, and to achieve their ground breaking results they had to ensemble several of these large models together to eke out tiny gains. In this paper I do a deep dive into N-BEATS architecture and I suggest several improvements.  I introduce a variety of novel block types using discrete wavelet transforms, DWTs, AutoEncoders, AEs, and AutoEncoders with latent sigmoid gates, which collaborate to achieve 60–95% reductions in parameter counts compared to the original NBEATS-G baselines while surpassing or equalling prior published results. In addition I show that the N-BEATS Generic blocks exhibit bimodal training collapse in 40–50% of runs under certain conditions while novel autoencoder-compressed variants with 10–50× fewer parameters converge reliably. I introduce an active_g parameter that "fixes" Generic block bimodal collapse and which can improve statistically signifigant accuracy on some datasets. I introduce a flexible stack architecture which allows for stacks constructed of any combination of availabe block types in any order. I introduce tiered frequency-band cascades using wavelet blocks using configurable orthonormal basis offsets, per stack, that creates a multi-scale decomposition, similar to NHiTS, across stacks that decisively improves accuracy on noisy high-frequency signals, (8 out of 10 of the M4-Daily top-10 use tiered wavelet stacks).  I also pair these wavelet blocks with AutoEncoder root blocks which shave parameters without adversely affecting accuracy in most instances. I also introduce novel TrendWavelet(AE, AELG, Root) blocks that combines a Trend basis with a Wavelet basis in the same Block thus saving parameters from using both block types individually within a stack. TrendWavelet blocks produce Sub-1M parameter configurations that achieve within 0.5% SMAPE of 19–43M parameter baselines models representing a 12–80× compression with no accuracy loss.N-BEATS' basis expansion scaffold is an effective structure for time series analysis, but the original  global Fourier basis choice for high frequency data doesn't take into account the localized frequency pertubations of non-stationary signals. Orthonormal wavelet bases combined with with autoencoder feature extractors are more accurate and efficient.
