# Wavelets and AutoEncoders: Parameter-Efficient Building Blocks for Reusable Basis Projections

## Abstract

N-BEATS, Oreshkin et al. (2019), broke new ground in univariate time series forecasting by being one of the first pure deep learning algorithms that was competitive with traditional statistical and hybrid statistical/deep learning techniques.  They pionered the use of neural basis expansion in which the network learns coefficients of a frozen basis. However, their solution came at an extreme cost, a single N-BEATS model could often reach 25M-45M parameters. In this paper I introduce a variety of novel block types using discrete wavelet transforms which provide localized crequency information in place of thte global polynomial basis used in original N-BEATS. Furthermore, I used AutoEncoders in a variety of configurations as filtered feature extractors to cut noise and to save parameters. My novel architecture improvements reduce parameter counts by 60–95% while surpassing or equalling prior published results without ensembiling. In addition I intriduce a nwew TrendWavelet block type which combining the low pass Trend basis with a configurable Wavelet basis that can be combined into sub 1M parameter models that score within 0.5% SMAPE of 19M-43M parameter basline models representing a 12-80x compression with no loss of accuracy. Furthremore, this construct and many other constructs in this paper are transferrable to other architectures like NHiTs and Transformers with equally impressive reductions in parameter counts with matched or improved accuracies. These transferrences are demonstrated with several experiments.

In addition I show that the N-BEATS Generic blocks exhibit bimodal training collapse in 40–50% of runs under certain conditions while novel autoencoder-compressed variants with 10–50× fewer parameters converge reliably. I introduce an active_g parameter that "fixes" Generic block bimodal collapse and which can improve statistically signifigant accuracy on some datasets. I introduce a flexible stack architecture which allows for stacks constructed of any combination of availabe block types in any order. I introduce tiered frequency-band cascades using wavelet blocks using configurable orthonormal basis offsets, per stack, that creates a multi-scale decomposition, similar to NHiTS, across stacks that decisively improves accuracy on noisy high-frequency signals, (8 out of 10 of the M4-Daily top-10 use tiered wavelet stacks).  I also pair these wavelet blocks with AutoEncoder root blocks which shave parameters without adversely affecting accuracy in most instances. I also introduce novel TrendWavelet(AE, AELG, Root) blocks that combines a Trend and a Wavelet basis in the same Block thus saving parameters from using both block types individually within a stack. TrendWavelet blocks produce Sub-1M parameter configurations that achieve within 0.5% SMAPE of 19–43M parameter baselines models representing a 12–80× compression with no accuracy loss.N-BEATS' basis expansion scaffold is an effective structure for time series analysis, but the original  global Fourier basis choice for high frequency data doesn't take into account the localized frequency pertubations of non-stationary signals. Orthonormal wavelet bases combined with with autoencoder feature extractors are more accurate and efficient.
